Career Plans

Comparing everyone’s plans in aiming for their ideal jobs, there are some common elements that match with other members. The most common plan was to continue studying, usually referring to courses in the university. While “Introduction to Information Technology” is often a course taken by students enrolled in Bachelor of IT, it is also an optional course that other members have chosen as an elective. Whatever course any member was in, their job majorly involved IT, so everyone’s plan involved their studies in the university as a core part of achieving their ideal jobs. Self-study also involves outside resources, such as free courses on the internet that give experience in various areas of IT related to their job.
Along with self-study and course studies, some members plan to apply for jobs and/or internships related to IT to gain experience in real-world scenarios. While general skills such as communication and teamwork, which are vital in a job setting, are common things that members wanted to work on, two members had specifically stated how they were going to face their improvement on general skills. One member is using the groups they work with in courses to practise leadership in teamwork scenarios, along with utilising university activities to improve communication skills, while another member plans to apply for public speaking classes to improve their general communication skills to large groups of people, as their job focuses on the business aspect of IT.
The ideal jobs that members aim for all correlate to the world of IT, resulting in similar skills being required, such as experience in a specific field of IT, and the ability to communicate effectively to clients and team members. 3 members had “Analyst” in their position name, but had varying requirements, as they involved skills specific to their job. One member had more general skills including “programming and scripting knowledge”, while another member required an “understanding of frameworks”. A majority of members required previous years of experience in their field for their ideal jobs, but some members only required one year of experience, or just general knowledge in their field for the position. Some members had goals that would specify the exact skills they would need, such as direct qualifications or knowledge in a specific brand of IT like “qualification in CBAP”, while few members had more general skills such as “problem solving” and “understanding of software languages”.
