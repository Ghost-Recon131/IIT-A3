Praniv
With assignment 3 we focused on being the most efficient we could by firstly creating mock-ups together to ensure everyone was on the same page, after which we split up the tasks with different people or pairs of members working on prototypes and frames, so more can be done. While this made it faster to get work done and less time was wasted, as while some were working on their frames others would be working on a different frame or prototype, the downside was that some members may be confused due to members not being involved in other prototypes or knowing how others function, which could have resulted in a contradiction of our frames. We did have a lot better communication in this assignment though, a lot better than assignment 2, everyone was active in talking with each other and ensures other members knew when problems arise.
Jingxuan
In assignment 3, we’ve made creating the design wireframes or first task as a group, so that we all have a starting point to work off. After that, we split up remaining artifacts & reports into parts to ensure no one repeats the same task. The split up of work also allowed us to stay ahead despite illnesses or other factors effecting team member’s ability to work. During this assignment we also improved our communications, and always kept the team informed on our work & if we are having trouble. We could have improved by having a few more meetings to keep track of tasks better as well as get members more involved in artefacts.

What we learned about groups
In relation to group a group work, we learnt that it can be difficult to work in one if the certain criteria and prerequisites are not set. Planning, setting deadlines and communication seem to be the key factors which attribute to successful group work. While we did not successfully adopt all these attributes, we did try and really improved from assignment 2 in terms of our communication and planning however we lacked in setting deadlines which really contradicted our planning. This is because planning is made redundant when the deadlines are not meant as it means we cannot progress until the work that is due is completed. This was not a major issue though as we did work on prototypes and frames separately however it is important to recognise this as it can be useful for future group work. We do believe however that this issue could be solved if groups pick a leader, or at least someone who will run things within the group, set deadlines and ensure the group is on track, as within our group we didn’t have a leader we mostly just got on calls and talked about what we were doing but we didn’t really lay down deadlines which a good leader would be able to do. An aspect of this leadership that would be even better is the idea of shared leadership in which members within the group are able to lead one another in an attempt to achieve the groups goals (Bligh, Pearce, Kohles, 2006), which would work greatly in group work as it could mean different members take leadership on sections in which they are the strongest at/ know the most about. We also realised that 6 members in a group while good due to the variety of ideas and differing skills can also be troublesome due to the variety of things that occur in each person’s life. These areas can impact the individual which in turn impacts the group, which is why we recognise that groups should have different plans in place so they can still progress if such an event occurs within the group. While some of our member had negative external factors influencing them, they still communicate very well and didn’t let it stop them from getting their allocated parts completed which they did so at a very high quality.
